Key Insight: Every day of CCC reduction frees working capital equal to daily revenue. Companies with negative CCC (Amazon, Dell) generate float income rather than bearing financing cost. Supply chain finance programs target DPO extension without harming suppliers.
Key Insight: Supply chain finance creates value through credit arbitrage between buyer's lower CoC and supplier's higher CoC. Dynamic discounting deploys buyer cash at returns above WACC. Reverse factoring lets suppliers access financing at the buyer's credit rating. Platforms like C2FO and Taulia implement this as real-time auctions.
Key Insight: Q* maximizes expected profit but not risk-adjusted profit. A risk-averse manager may order less than Q* to reduce VaR, trading expected profit for stability. This is the inventory analog of the mean-variance frontier in portfolio theory. In practice, ML models (XGBoost, neural nets) predict demand distributions from features, feeding Monte Carlo for data-driven inventory optimization.
